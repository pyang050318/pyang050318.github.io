# Research Experience
My research experience is centered on AI-driven perception, microscopic image understanding, object detection, and intelligent systems.

AI-based Meiotic Spindle Prediction in Human Oocytes

Project Leader · Nov. 2025 – Present

This project focuses on predicting and localizing meiotic spindles in human oocytes from brightfield and Hoffman microscopy images. Since spindles are often weakly visible or invisible in ordinary microscopy, the project explores cross-modal image generation, spindle-aware enhancement, object detection, and multi-result voting for clinically meaningful localization.

My work includes experimental design, model improvement, innovation proposal, video abstract preparation, patent and software copyright writing, and manuscript preparation.

Air-ground Cooperative Intelligent Perception Project

Undergraduate Leader · Jun. 2024 – Sep. 2024

This project involved video data labeling and classification, image quality enhancement using REAL-ESRGAN, trajectory recognition and tracking using YOLOv8, and autonomous obstacle avoidance and path planning with reinforcement learning.

Through this project, I improved my understanding of deep neural networks, GAN-based super-resolution reconstruction, code reproduction, reinforcement learning, and independent implementation.
