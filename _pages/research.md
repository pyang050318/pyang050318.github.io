# Research Experience

<div class="research-title">My research experience is centered on AI-driven perception, microscopic image understanding, object detection, and intelligent systems.</div>

<div class="research-card">
<h2>AI-based Meiotic Spindle Prediction in Human Oocytes</h2>
<div class="meta">Project Leader · Nov. 2025 – Present</div>
<p>This project focuses on predicting and localizing meiotic spindles in human oocytes from brightfield and Hoffman microscopy images. Since spindles are often weakly visible or invisible in ordinary microscopy, the project explores cross-modal image generation, spindle-aware enhancement, object detection, and multi-result voting for clinically meaningful localization.</p>
<p>My work includes experimental design, model improvement, innovation proposal, video abstract preparation, patent and software copyright writing, and manuscript preparation.</p>
<span class="badge">Medical Image Analysis</span><span class="badge">Microscopy</span><span class="badge">Diffusion Models</span><span class="badge">Object Detection</span><span class="badge">Clinical Localization</span>

  <li><strong>ASRM video abstract:</strong> <em>Spindle Prediction of Human Oocytes Using Artificial Intelligence From Brightfield Images</em>, accepted, second author.</li>
  <li><strong>Manuscript:</strong> <em>Spindle-aware Cross-modal Diffusion for Meiotic Spindle Localization in Human Oocytes From Hoffman Modulation Contrast Microscopy</em>, in preparation.</li>
